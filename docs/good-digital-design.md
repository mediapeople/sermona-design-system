<div class="sermona-hero-split">
<div>
<p class="sermona-kicker">Dynamics · Not decoration</p>
<h1 class="sermona-display-light" style="font-size: clamp(2.25rem, 6vw, var(--sermona-font-size-display-megaman)); margin: 0;">Good digital<br/>design moves</h1>
</div>
<aside class="sermona-hero-split__aside">
<p class="sermona-lede" style="max-width: none;">Interfaces succeed when <strong>type, space, color, and motion</strong> agree on a single story. The “dynamics” are the relationships between those choices: what leads, what recedes, and what answers the user back.</p>
</aside>
</div>

</section>

<hr class="sermona-rule" style="max-width: var(--sermona-layout-max-full); margin: 0 auto var(--sermona-space-4);" />

<section class="sermona-section" style="padding-inline: var(--sermona-space-5); max-width: var(--sermona-layout-max-full); margin: 0 auto;">
<header class="sermona-section__header" style="max-width: var(--sermona-layout-max-wide);">
<p class="sermona-kicker">Four forces</p>
<p class="sermona-lede">Most screens fail from competing centers of attention. Strong digital design channels eye and intent along a path: one primary action, supporting detail, and honest feedback.</p>
</header>

<div class="sermona-service-grid" style="max-width: var(--sermona-layout-max-full);">
<article class="sermona-icon-tile">
<div class="sermona-icon-tile__glyph" aria-hidden="true"><span class="sermona-icon"><svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.5"><path d="M4 6h16M4 12h10M4 18h16"/></svg></span></div>
<h3 class="sermona-icon-tile__title">Hierarchy &amp; intent</h3>
<p class="sermona-icon-tile__body">Scale and weight tell users what matters first. When everything shouts, nothing is heard — tune display, heading, and body roles and stick to them.</p>
</article>
<article class="sermona-icon-tile">
<div class="sermona-icon-tile__glyph" aria-hidden="true"><span class="sermona-icon"><svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.5"><rect x="3" y="3" width="18" height="18" rx="2"/><path d="M9 9h6v6H9z"/></svg></span></div>
<h3 class="sermona-icon-tile__title">Space &amp; rhythm</h3>
<p class="sermona-icon-tile__body">Whitespace is not emptiness; it is tempo. Consistent spacing lets people scan, breathe, and predict where the next beat lands.</p>
</article>
<article class="sermona-icon-tile">
<div class="sermona-icon-tile__glyph" aria-hidden="true"><span class="sermona-icon"><svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.5"><circle cx="12" cy="12" r="3"/><path d="M12 2v4M12 18v4M4.93 4.93l2.83 2.83M16.24 16.24l2.83 2.83M2 12h4M18 12h4"/></svg></span></div>
<h3 class="sermona-icon-tile__title">Contrast &amp; focus</h3>
<p class="sermona-icon-tile__body">Legibility and accessibility start with color relationships. Reserve the strongest contrast for primary content and actions; use muted tones for supporting copy.</p>
</article>
<article class="sermona-icon-tile">
<div class="sermona-icon-tile__glyph" aria-hidden="true"><span class="sermona-icon"><svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="1.5"><path d="M12 2L2 7l10 5 10-5-10-5z"/><path d="M2 17l10 5 10-5"/><path d="M2 12l10 5 10-5"/></svg></span></div>
<h3 class="sermona-icon-tile__title">Systems &amp; reuse</h3>
<p class="sermona-icon-tile__body">Good design compounds: tokens, components, and patterns let teams ship faster without drifting off-brand. One source of truth beats one-off heroics.</p>
</article>
</div>

</section>

<section class="sermona-section sermona-section--loose" style="padding-inline: var(--sermona-space-5); max-width: var(--sermona-layout-max-full); margin: 0 auto;">

<div class="sermona-two-col" style="align-items: center;">

<div class="sermona-stack sermona-stack--loose">
<p class="sermona-kicker">Feedback loops</p>
<p class="sermona-h3" style="margin: 0;">The interface should acknowledge every meaningful action</p>
<p class="sermona-body">Loading, success, and error states are part of the design — not polish added later. Motion should clarify state changes (short, purposeful) rather than perform for its own sake.</p>
<ul class="sermona-feature-list" style="margin-top: var(--sermona-space-3);">
<li class="sermona-feature-list__item">
<span class="sermona-feature-list__icon" aria-hidden="true"><span class="sermona-icon"><svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2"><polyline points="20 6 9 17 4 12"/></svg></span></span>
<div>
<p class="sermona-feature-list__title">Progressive disclosure</p>
<p class="sermona-feature-list__body">Reveal complexity only when context calls for it; keep first screens calm and decisive.</p>
</div>
</li>
<li class="sermona-feature-list__item">
<span class="sermona-feature-list__icon" aria-hidden="true"><span class="sermona-icon"><svg viewBox="0 0 24 24" fill="none" stroke="currentColor" stroke-width="2"><path d="M12 22s8-4 8-10V5l-8-3-8 3v7c0 6 8 10 8 10z"/></svg></span></span>
<div>
<p class="sermona-feature-list__title">Resilient defaults</p>
<p class="sermona-feature-list__body">Accessible labels, focus order, and predictable patterns protect users when they’re tired, distracted, or on a small screen.</p>
</div>
</li>
</ul>
</div>

<blockquote class="sermona-quote">
<p class="sermona-quote__text">“Design is not how it looks — it is how it behaves under stress: slow networks, edge cases, and real human rhythm.”</p>
<p class="sermona-quote__cite">—Digital craft</p>
</blockquote>
